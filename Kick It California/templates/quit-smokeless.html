Serious Health Risks

What Smokeless Tobacco Does

Just because you're not smoking doesn't mean it's safe. Smokeless tobacco causes serious health problems.

👄

Oral Cancer

  • Cancer of the mouth, tongue, cheek, and gums
  • 50 times higher risk than non-users
  • Can require surgery, radiation, chemotherapy
  • May result in disfigurement or death
🦷

Gum & Tooth Damage

  • Receding gums that expose tooth roots
  • Tooth decay and tooth loss
  • Bone loss around teeth
  • Permanent staining and bad breath
❤️

Heart Disease

  • Increased heart rate and blood pressure
  • Higher risk of heart attack and stroke
  • Damage to blood vessels
  • Irregular heartbeat
🧠

Nicotine Addiction

  • More nicotine absorbed than cigarettes
  • Strong physical and psychological dependence
  • Difficult withdrawal symptoms
  • Impacts brain development in young users

Quit Strategies

How to Quit Smokeless Tobacco

Quitting chew and dip requires specific strategies. Here's what works.

📅

Pick a Quit Date

Choose a specific date within 2 weeks. Pick a low-stress day. Mark it on your calendar and tell people about it.

🚮

Throw It All Out

Get rid of all tobacco products, tins, and spittoons. Clean your truck, locker, and anywhere you kept dip.

💊

Use Nicotine Replacement

Nicotine gum, lozenges, or patches can help manage withdrawal. They give you nicotine without the harmful chemicals.

🔄

Find Substitutes

Stock up on sunflower seeds, gum, mints, or herbal snuff. Have multiple options ready for different situations.

⏰

Break Your Routine

If you always dip while driving or fishing, change your routine. Take a different route, try a new activity.

💪

Stay Active

Exercise reduces cravings and stress. Even a 10-minute walk can help you get through a strong craving.

👥

Get Support

Talk to a Quit Coach who understands smokeless tobacco addiction. Join online support groups with people who get it.

🎯

Track Your Savings

Calculate how much you're saving. A can-a-day habit costs $900-1,500 per year. Watch your savings grow!
