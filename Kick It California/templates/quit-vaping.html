Truth vs Fiction

Vaping Myths Debunked

Get the facts about vaping and e-cigarettes from real science, not marketing.

❌

Vaping is harmless water vapor

E-cigarette aerosol contains harmful chemicals including heavy metals, volatile organic compounds, and cancer-causing agents.

Studies show vaping can damage your lungs, heart, and brain development in young people.

❌

Vapes help you quit smoking

Most people who vape to quit smoking end up using both products (dual use), doubling their nicotine intake.

FDA-approved quit methods like coaching and NRT are proven more effective.

❌

Vaping isn't addictive

Vapes deliver highly concentrated nicotine - one JUUL pod equals a pack of cigarettes. Nicotine is extremely addictive.

Teen vaping has created a new generation addicted to nicotine.

❌

Flavors make vapes safer

Flavoring chemicals can be toxic when inhaled. Some flavors contain diacetyl, linked to serious lung disease.

Flavors are used to make vaping more appealing, especially to youth.

Health Risks

What Vaping Does to Your Body

The long-term effects are still emerging, but here's what we know now.

🫁

Lung Damage

Causes inflammation, reduced lung function, and increases risk of respiratory infections. "Popcorn lung" and EVALI are serious conditions.

🧠

Brain Development

Nicotine harms brain development in people under 25, affecting attention, learning, and mood control.

❤️

Heart Problems

Increases heart rate and blood pressure, raises risk of heart attack and stroke, damages blood vessels.

🦷

Oral Health

Causes gum disease, tooth decay, dry mouth, and increases risk of oral cancer.

⚡

Addiction

High nicotine levels create strong addiction, leading to cravings, withdrawal, and difficulty quitting.

🤰

Pregnancy Risks

Nicotine exposure during pregnancy can harm fetal brain and lung development.

Quit Strategies

How to Quit Vaping Successfully

Proven strategies tailored specifically for e-cigarette and vape users.

1

Track Your Usage

Use an app or journal to track when and why you vape. Identifying patterns helps you prepare alternatives for triggers.

2

Gradual Reduction

Slowly decrease nicotine strength or number of puffs per day. Set weekly goals and stick to them.

3

Delete Reminders

Remove vape-related apps, unfollow vape accounts on social media, and get rid of all vape products and accessories.

4

Avoid Triggers

If you vape while stressed, find healthier alternatives: exercise, meditation, deep breathing, or talk to someone.

5

Stay Hydrated

Drink lots of water to help flush nicotine from your system and reduce cravings. Keep a water bottle handy.

6

Find Alternatives

Keep your hands and mouth busy with gum, mints, fidget toys, or healthy snacks like carrots or sunflower seeds.

7

Get Support

Tell friends and family you're quitting. Join online support groups or talk to a Quit Coach who understands vaping addiction.

8

Manage Withdrawal

Withdrawal symptoms peak in 3-5 days and improve after 2 weeks. Exercise, good sleep, and coaching help you get through it.
