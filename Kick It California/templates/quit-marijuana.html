Understanding Dependence

Signs You Might Need Support

Cannabis use disorder is real. Here are signs that marijuana may be affecting your life.

🧠

Daily Life Impact

  • Using more often or larger amounts than intended
  • Difficulty controlling or cutting back on use
  • Spending significant time obtaining or using
  • Cravings or strong urges to use
  • Failing to fulfill work, school, or home obligations
👥

Social & Activities

  • Giving up activities you once enjoyed
  • Continued use despite relationship problems
  • Using in physically hazardous situations
  • Social isolation or withdrawing from others
  • Conflicts with family or friends about use
❤️

Physical & Mental Health

  • Using despite knowing it causes health problems
  • Needing more to achieve the same effect (tolerance)
  • Experiencing withdrawal symptoms when stopping
  • Memory and concentration problems
  • Increased anxiety or depression
💰

Work & Finances

  • Financial strain from purchasing cannabis
  • Job performance or attendance issues
  • Failing drug tests or fear of testing
  • Missing opportunities due to use
  • Legal problems related to use
What to Expect

Withdrawal & Recovery Timeline

Understanding what happens when you quit helps you prepare and stay strong.

Days 1-3: Peak Withdrawal

Common symptoms: Irritability, anxiety, insomnia, decreased appetite, headaches, restlessness, mood swings.

What helps: Stay hydrated, light exercise, relaxation techniques, talk to a coach, remind yourself this is temporary.

Days 4-14: Symptoms Improve

Common symptoms: Continued cravings, vivid dreams, mood fluctuations, concentration difficulties.

What helps: Establish new routines, stay busy, connect with support system, practice self-care, celebrate small wins.

Weeks 2-4: Finding Balance

What's happening: Physical symptoms largely gone, mental clarity returns, sleep improves, appetite normalizes.

Focus on: Building new habits, addressing triggers, finding healthy coping strategies, enjoying increased energy.

1+ Months: Long-term Success

Benefits continue: Better memory and focus, improved mood stability, more motivation, clearer thinking, deeper sleep.

Stay vigilant: Occasional cravings may occur during stress. Have a plan for high-risk situations.

Quit Strategies

8 Ways to Successfully Quit Cannabis

Evidence-based strategies that help you quit and stay quit.

1

Identify Your Why

Write down your reasons for quitting: job, health, relationships, finances, personal growth. Review this list when cravings hit.

2

Remove All Cannabis

Get rid of all marijuana, vape pens, edibles, paraphernalia, and anything associated with use. Make it harder to access.

3

Avoid Triggers

Know your triggers: people, places, times, emotions. Plan ahead for how you'll handle them. Change your routine.

4

Find New Activities

Fill the time you spent using with healthy activities: exercise, hobbies, social events, learning something new.

5

Manage Stress Differently

Learn healthy coping skills: deep breathing, meditation, yoga, journaling, talking to someone, physical activity.

6

Build a Support Network

Tell trusted friends and family. Join support groups. Work with a Quit Coach. Connect with others who understand.

7

Take Care of Yourself

Prioritize sleep, nutrition, and exercise. Your body is healing. Give it what it needs to recover and feel better.

8

Plan for Slip-Ups

If you use again, don't give up. Learn from it. What triggered it? How can you handle it differently next time?
